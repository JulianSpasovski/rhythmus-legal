Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Rhythmus handles information in the current iOS beta version.

Home Support

Summary

Rhythmus is designed as a personal training planning and workout logging app. In the current version reviewed for TestFlight readiness, training data is stored on the device in the app's local storage. The app does not use a developer-operated backend account system.

There are user-controlled exceptions: you may export or share app data, email support, enable Apple Health workout writing, or paste copied content into another app. Apple may also process diagnostics, TestFlight feedback, Health, device, or system-model information under Apple's own terms and settings.

Information You Enter or Generate

Rhythmus may store training plans, workout blocks, exercises, logged sets, rest information, workout history, problem reports, local training context, settings, preferences, and app-generated suggestions or summaries.

If you import text or screenshots, the selected content may be processed locally to help create or review a training plan. You decide whether reviewed import results are saved.

Local Storage

The current version stores app data locally using SwiftData and app-group storage so the app, widgets, and Live Activities can show relevant workout information. Settings may be stored in UserDefaults/AppStorage.

Temporary export files can be created when you choose to export data. These files may include training plans, workouts, problem reports, local training context, and settings. Rhythmus attempts to clean up temporary export files after sharing and during later app use.

Information Sent Off Device

The repository audit for this version did not find an app-owned backend, account server, analytics SDK, advertising SDK, crash-reporting SDK, or external API client. Rhythmus does not automatically send training data to a developer-controlled server in the reviewed version.

Information may leave your device when you choose to export or share files, send an email to support, paste copied content into another app, enable Apple Health workout writing, submit TestFlight feedback, or use Apple system services. Those destinations are controlled by you, Apple, your email provider, or the third-party app or service you choose.

Apple Health

Rhythmus can write completed strength workouts to Apple Health only after you enable the feature and grant permission. The reviewed version does not read Apple Health data. Workouts saved to Apple Health are managed through Apple's Health app, iCloud, device, and account settings.

Apple On-Device Intelligence

Rhythmus may use Apple's on-device Foundation Models or system language model features when available, with rule-based fallbacks. The repository audit did not find a developer-hosted AI service or external AI API. Apple system features may be governed by Apple's terms, privacy settings, and device capabilities.

Third-Party Services

The reviewed version does not include third-party analytics, advertising, tracking, payment, authentication, or crash-reporting SDKs. If you choose to paste copied content into ChatGPT or another external tool, or share exports through another app or service, that is user-initiated and handled by the selected third party.

Analytics, Advertising, Tracking, and Crash Reporting

The reviewed version does not include advertising SDKs, analytics SDKs, tracking permission, tracking domains, or a developer-controlled crash-reporting SDK. Apple and TestFlight may provide diagnostics or feedback information under Apple's own terms and user settings.

Accounts, Purchases, and Subscriptions

The reviewed version does not include an app account system, Sign in with Apple, in-app purchases, or subscriptions.

Permissions

Rhythmus may ask for Apple Health write permission if you enable workout saving. The app may process screenshots or files that you explicitly select for import. The reviewed version did not show camera, microphone, location, contacts, calendar, or tracking permission requests.

Data Retention and Deletion

Data stored by Rhythmus remains on your device until you delete it, reset app data, remove the app, or replace it through your own import/export choices. The in-app reset removes local Rhythmus data and settings from the app's storage. It does not automatically delete workouts already saved to Apple Health, emails you sent to support, TestFlight feedback, device diagnostics, or files you exported to other locations.

If you email support, the message and any attachments you provide may be retained as needed to respond, troubleshoot, and keep basic support records.

Health and Fitness Disclaimer

Rhythmus is a personal training, logging, and planning tool. It is not a regulated medical device, diagnostic product, treatment tool, injury-prevention product, medical monitoring product, or source of medical advice.

Children's Privacy

Rhythmus is not intended to be directed to children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through a support request, contact support so the request can be reviewed.

Your Choices

  • You can choose whether to save workouts to Apple Health.
  • You can choose whether to export or share app data.
  • You can choose what information to include in support emails.
  • You can reset local Rhythmus data in the app settings.
